## Srishty Shree — Portfolio Case Study

### Title
**Customer Retention Strategy via RFM Segmentation: Reallocating £1.4M of Annual Marketing Spend in a UK Online Retailer**

### One-Line Summary
Translated 1.07 million raw retail transactions into a five-action retention plan projected to defend or recover £1.35M–£1.45M in annual revenue at under 1% marketing spend.

### Stakeholder & Context
The decision-maker was the **Head of Marketing & Customer Retention** of a UK-based online gift retailer serving 5,861 active customers across 41 countries. The retention team was treating all customers identically — diluting marketing budget on low-value customers while letting high-value ones slip toward churn.

### Dataset
**UCI Online Retail II** — 1,067,371 raw transactions over Dec 2009 – Dec 2011, 8 columns. Cleaned to 776,641 valid line-items, £17.1M total revenue, 36,639 invoices.

### Approach
1. **Python ETL pipeline** to clean missing CustomerIDs (~25%), cancellations, returns, and non-product line items
2. **RFM scoring** on a 1–5 quintile scale, mapped to six business segments
3. **K-Means validation** of segment separation (>80% overlap with rule-based RFM)
4. **Tableau Public dashboard** with four interactive views and segment/country/time filters
5. **Insight synthesis** into prioritized recommendations with quantified impact

### Three Headline Findings
1. Champions are 25.2% of customers but generate 69.2% (£11.8M) of revenue — extreme Pareto concentration.
2. The At Risk segment (the largest by count, 26.1%) holds £1.93M of recoverable revenue.
3. International customers earn 4–6× higher revenue per customer than UK customers.

### Recommendations & Impact
| Action | Year-1 Net Impact |
|---|---|
| Champion Protection Program | +£485k–£530k |
| At Risk Win-Back Campaign | +£260k–£275k |
| Reallocate Lost-segment budget | +£20k–£30k |
| International premium expansion | +£50k–£70k |
| Second-purchase + Big Spender upsell | +£530k–£550k |
| **Total** | **+£1.35M–£1.45M** |
